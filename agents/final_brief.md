# MAMILAS — PRIVATE MESSAGE ESCAPE

## Concept

A man receives a suggestive message in a crowded café and assumes it must be opened in total privacy. He treats that minor need as a grave operational problem: he leaves the city, crosses snow, crosses desert, opens a portal, and enters an impossibly benign physical fantasy landscape. Only there does he open the message and discover a course promotion. The comedy comes from disproportionate effort, exact visual continuity, and a restrained performance — never from mugging.

## Visual contract

- Format: vertical 9:16 social film.
- Real-world register: precision-controlled photoreal commercial imagery; deep focus, squared geometry, locked or programmed camera, motivated practical light, restrained steel-teal shadows and warm amber highlights, accurate skin.
- Destination register: a photoreal live-action physical fantasy landscape, built from monumental practical-set terrain, real mineral, vegetation, water and atmospheric light. The world is surreal in colour and form but never animated, clay-rendered, cartoon or mobile-game-like. @3d is its sole environment authority.
- The portal changes the location, not the man's render register. @karakter remains fully photoreal live-action on both sides.
- Comedy performance: the character behaves as if privacy is a serious mission. Tiny eye movements and guarded posture; no broad reaction faces.
- Continuity: @karakter is the photoreal identity authority; @telefon is the phone-geometry authority; @3d is the destination environment, portal, palette, material and lighting authority. The man is never converted into an animated character.
- No visible brands or logos, except artwork already contained inside the user-approved @promosyon reference in shot 8. That approved artwork is reproduced exactly and is the only brand exception.
- Sequential visual references: tag approved shot 3 as @kar_gecidi before generating shot 4; tag approved shot 6 as @ucboyutlu before generating shots 7-9.
- The promotion is not invented. Shot 8 remains blocked until the user supplies and tags the approved artwork as @promosyon.
